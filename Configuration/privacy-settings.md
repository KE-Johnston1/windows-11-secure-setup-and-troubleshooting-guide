# Windows 11 Privacy Settings

## Purpose
A technician‑friendly guide for configuring Windows 11 privacy settings to reduce data collection, limit unnecessary telemetry, and protect user information.

---

## 1. General Privacy Controls
- Disable **Advertising ID**
- Disable **Tailored Experiences**
- Disable **Suggested Content** in Settings
- Disable **Typing and Inking Personalisation**

---

## 2. App Permissions
Review and disable permissions that are not required:

### Location
- Disable location services (optional)
- Restrict location access to essential apps only

### Camera & Microphone
- Disable for apps that do not require access
- Confirm permissions for conferencing tools (Zoom, Teams)

### Notifications
- Disable notifications for unnecessary apps

### Background Apps
- Disable background activity for non‑essential apps

---

## 3. Diagnostic Data
- Set Diagnostic Data to **Required Only**
- Disable **Improve Inking & Typing**
- Disable **Tailored Experiences**
- Disable **Feedback Frequency** (set to “Never”)

---

## 4. Online Privacy
- Disable **Sync across devices** (optional)
- Disable **Search highlights**
- Disable **Cloud clipboard** (optional)
- Disable **Activity history**

---

## Technician Notes
- Privacy settings vary by environment — enterprise policies may override local settings  
- Some features (e.g., location) may be required for specific apps  
- Document any changes for compliance or rollback
